# Differentiators

**Differentiators** are distinct, unique qualities, features, or benefits that set a business, product, or individual apart from competitors. They answer the fundamental question: Why should someone choose you over the alternatives? [1, 2, 3, 4]  

A true, effective differentiator should pass three core tests: 

* it must be true (you can prove it)
* relevant (it matters to your audience)
* defensible (competitors cannot easily copy or match it) [5, 6]  

## Common Types of Differentiators 

* Proprietary Technology or Patents: Unique software capabilities, exclusive materials, or intellectual property. 
* Specialized Niche: Focusing exclusively on a very specific industry, demographic, or localized problem. 
* Customer Experience: Premium support, faster turnaround times, or an exceptionally high-touch, personalized buying journey. 
* Proven Track Record: Impressive, highly recognizable client lists or quantifiable results and case studies. 
* Innovative Business Model: A unique pricing structure (e.g., subscription-based, freemium) or a specialized delivery method. [5, 6, 7, 8, 9]  

## How to Identify and Build Yours 

1. Analyze Competitors: Identify what your competitors typically promote and look for the gaps they are ignoring. 
2. Gather Customer Feedback: Ask your best customers exactly why they chose you and what they value most about your service or product. 
3. Audit Your "Superpowers": Look at your team's unique skill sets, your company's history, or your operational strengths. [4, 5]
