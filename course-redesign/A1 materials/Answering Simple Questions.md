# Answering Simple Questions (Répondre à des questions simples)

**PROMPT 1:** The DELF A1 test says a student should be able to "answer simple personal questions." Can you tell me what that means exactly?

**RESPONSE 1:** Answering simple personal questions at the DELF A1 level means you can provide basic, everyday information about yourself using short, formulaic sentences. [1]  

Here is exactly what you need to be able to talk about, broken down by category. 

## Personal Identity 

 * Name: State your first and last name. 
 * Age: Say how old you are. 
 * Nationality: Name your country of origin. 
 * Birthday: Give your date of birth. 

## Contact Information 

 * Address: State where you currently live. 
 * Phone number: Say or spell out your phone number. 
 * Email: Spell out your email address. [2]  

## Daily Life & Background 

 * Profession: State your job or say you are a student. 
 * Languages: Name the languages you speak. 
 * Family: Mention if you are married or have children. 
 * Housing: Describe if you live in a house or apartment. 

## Tastes & Preferences 

 * Hobbies: List activities you like to do. 
 * Sports: Name sports you play. 
 * Likes: State your favorite foods, colors, or music. 
 * Dislikes: Express basic things you do not enjoy. 

## Typical Question & Answer Examples 

| Examiner Question [3] | Your Expected Answer  |
| --- | --- |
| Comment tu t'appelles ? (What is your name?) | Je m'appelle Thomas. (My name is Thomas.)  |
| Quel âge as-tu ? (How old are you?) | J'ai 25 ans. (I am 25 years old.)  |
| Où habites-tu ? (Where do you live?) | J'habite à Tokyo. (I live in Tokyo.)  |
| Quelle est ta profession ? (What is your job?) | Je suis ingénieur. (I am an engineer.)  |
| Qu'est-ce que tu aimes faire ? (What do you like to do?) | J'aime lire et nager. (I like to read and swim.)  |

## Key Skills Required to Pass 

 * Understand question words: Recognize où (where), quand (when), comment (how), and qui (who). 
 * Spell aloud: Know the French alphabet to spell your name or email. 
 * Count: Know numbers 0–100 for your age, phone number, and address. 
 * Use basic verbs: Conjugate être (to be), avoir (to have), habiter (to live), and aimer (to like) in the present tense. [4, 5]
