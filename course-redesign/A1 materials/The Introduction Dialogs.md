# The Introduction Dialogs (Les dialogues d'introduction)

**PROMPT:** Could you give me a few sample back-and-forth dialogs in French that my wife and I could practice with? Perhaps formal and an informal introductions as spoken by a woman and a man? And maybe a couple different examples so I'll be prepared for a trip to France? I assume not everyone says the same exact thing every time.

**RESPONSE:** Practice these four diverse French introduction dialogs with your wife to prepare for different social scenarios during your trip.

French speakers alter their vocabulary based on social distance (formal vous vs. informal tu) and gender, which affects adjectives like "enchanté" (masculine) and "enchantée" (feminine).


## Scenario 1: Informal Introduction (Casual / Meeting Peers)
Use this with people your own age in casual settings like a café, a tour group, or a social event. It uses tu and informal greetings.

* Man: Salut ! Moi c'est Marc. Et toi ? (Hi! I'm Marc. And you?)
* Woman: Salut Marc ! Je m'appelle Sophie. (Hi Marc! My name is Sophie.)
* Man: Enchanté, Sophie. Tu es de Paris ? (Nice to meet you, Sophie. Are you from Paris?)
* Woman: Non, je suis américaine, de New York. Et toi ? (No, I'm American, from New York. And you?)
* Man: Je suis canadien. Bienvenue en France ! (I'm Canadian. Welcome to France!)
* Woman: Merci ! Ravie de te rencontrer. (Thank you! Delighted to meet you.)

## Scenario 2: Formal Introduction (Business / Respectful)
Use this with hotel staff, restaurant hosts, older individuals, or in professional settings. It uses vous and formal structures.

* Woman: Bonjour Monsieur. Je me présente, Sarah Miller. (Bonjour Sir. Let me introduce myself, Sarah Miller.)
* Man: Bonjour Madame. Jean Dubois. Enchanté. (Bonjour Ma'am. Jean Dubois. Nice to meet you.)
* Woman: Comment allez-vous, M. Dubois ? (How are you, Mr. Dubois?)
* Man: Je vais très bien, merci. Et vous-même ? (I am doing very well, thank you. And yourself?)
* Woman: Très bien, merci. C'est mon premier voyage en France. (Very well, thank you. This is my first trip to France.)
* Man: Ah, merveilleux ! Je vous souhaite un excellent séjour. (Ah, wonderful! I wish you an excellent stay.)

## Scenario 3: At a Restaurant or Shop (The "Client" Interaction)
When traveling, your most common introductions will be quick, polite check-ins with service staff.

* Man (Server): Bonjour Madame, bonjour Monsieur. Bienvenue au Bistro. (Hello Ma'am, hello Sir. Welcome to the Bistro.)
* Woman (Guest): Bonjour. Nous avons une réservation au nom de Miller. (Hello. We have a reservation under the name Miller.)
* Man (Server): Parfait. Installez-vous ici. Vous désirez un apéritif ? (Perfect. Have a seat here. Would you like an aperitif?)
* Woman (Guest): Oui, s'il vous plaît. Deux verres de vin blanc. (Yes, please. Two glasses of white wine.)

## Scenario 4: Asking for Help (The Polite Opening)
In France, it is considered impolite to ask a question without a proper greeting first. Always start with Bonjour.

* Woman (Traveler): Pardon Monsieur, bonjour. Excusez-moi de vous déranger. (Pardon me Sir, hello. Excuse me for bothering you.)
* Man (Local): Bonjour Madame. Pas de problème. Je vous écoute. (Hello Ma'am. No problem. I'm listening.)
* Woman (Traveler): Est-ce que vous parlez anglais ? Nous cherchons le musée. (Do you speak English? We are looking for the museum.)
* Man (Local): Un petit peu, oui ! Le musée est juste là-bas, à droite. (A little bit, yes! The museum is just over there, to the right.)
* Woman (Traveler): Ah, super ! Merci beaucoup. Bonne journée ! (Ah, great! Thank you very much. Have a good day!)
* Man (Local): De rien. Bonne journée à vous aussi ! (You're welcome. Have a good day too!)

## Key Grammar Quirks to Notice

* The Extra "E": When writing, a woman writes Enchantée or Ravie, while a man writes Enchanté or Ravi. They sound exactly the same out loud.
* Moi c'est...: This means "As for me, I'm...". It is a highly common, modern, and natural way to introduce yourself casually instead of always repeating Je m'appelle.
* Je vous prie / S'il vous plaît: Always stick to the plural/formal vous forms with strangers to show respect.
